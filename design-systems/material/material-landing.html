Design Principles

Material Design philosophy in practice

🎨

Material is the Metaphor

Surfaces and shadows provide meaning about hierarchy and interaction. Physical properties guide user understanding.

✨

Bold, Graphic, Intentional

Deliberate color choices, edge-to-edge imagery, and large-scale typography create focus and hierarchy.

⚡

Motion Provides Meaning

Responsive animation reinforces user actions. Transitions are purposeful, not decorative.

🌐

Cross-Platform

Consistent experience across devices and platforms while respecting platform conventions.

♿

Accessible by Default

Strong contrast ratios, clear focus states, and semantic HTML ensure usability for everyone.

🔧

Flexible & Scalable

Design tokens and component patterns that adapt to any brand while maintaining Material principles.

What to Avoid

Common anti-patterns that break Material Design principles

🚫

Off-Palette Colors

Do not introduce colors outside the defined token system. Use existing tokens to maintain consistency.

🚫

Flat Hierarchy

Do not use the same type size and weight for all text. Clear hierarchy is essential for scanability.

🚫

Decorative Effects

Do not add effects that reduce readability or accessibility. Every visual element should serve a purpose.

🚫

Mixed Metaphors

Do not mix unrelated visual metaphors in the same interface. Maintain consistent design language.
