Design Principles

Core principles that guide the Retro design system

High Contrast

Bold colors and strong contrasts ensure readability and create visual impact reminiscent of vintage computing displays.

Clear Hierarchy

Obvious visual hierarchy with headline → support text → primary action flow keeps interfaces intuitive.

Consistent Spacing

Predictable spacing scale maintains vertical rhythm and creates harmonious layouts across all components.

Nostalgic Elements

Chunky borders, bold shadows, and retro color palettes evoke the golden age of personal computing.

Anti-patterns to Avoid

Keep your retro designs authentic and usable

❌ Off-Palette Colors

Do not introduce colors outside the defined palette. Existing tokens solve most problems.

❌ Flat Hierarchy

Do not use the same type size and weight for all text. Hierarchy must be obvious.

❌ Decorative Overload

Do not add effects that reduce readability or accessibility. Function over decoration.

❌ Mixed Metaphors

Do not mix unrelated visual metaphors in the same interface. Stay consistent.
